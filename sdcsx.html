Seven Wonders of the World

General Introduction

The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, also known as the Seven Wonders, is a list of seven notable structures present during classical antiquity...

Instead of "wonders", the ancient Greeks spoke of "theamata", which means "sights", in other words "things to be seen"...

Another ancient writer, who, perhaps dubiously, identified himself as Philo of Byzantium, wrote a short account entitled The Seven Sights of the World...

The list covered only the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions, which then comprised the known world for the Greeks...

The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

  • Great Pyramid of Giza
  • Hanging Gardens of Babylon
  • Statue of Zeus at Olympia
  • Temple of Artemis at Ephesus
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus
  • Colossus of Rhodes
  • Lighthouse of Alexandria
